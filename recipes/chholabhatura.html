Chhola Bhatura

Famous Delhi Chhola Bhatura

Chhola Bhatura is most common daily food for commuters in streets of Delhi and Punjab.

Ingredients

    For Bhature with Yeast

  • 1½ cup Refined flour
  • ½ tsp Sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • ½ tsp Oil
  • 5 gms Dry Yeast soaked in water & sugar
  • Water
  • 2 tbsp Semolina, soaked in water
  • 1 tsp Oil

    • For Pressure Cooking Chole

  • 1 ½ cups Chickpeas, soaked, overnight
  • 4-5 Dry Amla
  • 1 Dry red chilli
  • 2 Black cardamom
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp Baking soda
  • 1 Bay leaf
  • 2 tbsp Tea powder
  • Water as required

    • For Chole Masala

  • 2-4 Black cardamom
  • 10-12 Black peppercorns
  • 2-3 Green cardamom
  • 2 Mace
  • ½ tbsp Dry fenugreek leaves
  • 1 inch Cinnamon stick
  • ½ Nutmeg
  • 1 Star anise
  • 2-4 Cloves
  • ¼ tsp Fenugreek seeds
  • 1 tbsp Coriander powder
  • A pinch of asafoetida
  • ½ tsp Degi red chilli powder
  • ½ tsp Cumin powder

    • For Tempering Chole

  • ¼ cup Ghee
  • Prepared Chole Masala
  • 5 tbsp Black tamarind water, soaked
  • ½ cup Remaining chole water
  • 1 inch Ginger, julienned
  • 2 tbsp Ghee

    • For Fried Aloo

  • 2 medium Potatoes, boiled & cubed
  • Oil for frying
  • Salt to taste
  • ½ tsp Degi red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp Dry mango powder

    • For Garnish

  • 1 medium Onion, slice
  • 2 fresh Green chillies
  • ½ inch Ginger, julienned
  • Green chutney
  • Few fresh Coriander sprig

    Steps

    For Bhature With Yeast

  1. In a parat, add refined flour, sugar, salt to taste, oil, dry yeast soaked in water, semolina soaked in water for 1 hour and mix well making sure everything is well combined.
  2. Further, add water as required and knead the dough.
  3. Knead to the smooth and soft dough without putting much pressure.
  4. Grease the dough with oil, cover and rest for 1 hours.
  5. Pinch a ball sized dough and make a ball without any cracks.
  6. Roll slightly thick, applying oil to prevent it from sticking.
  7. Cover and rest for 15 minutes.
  8. Finally, roll the dough evenly into circles using a rolling pin. roll neither too thin or thick.
  9. Heat oil in a deep kadai. When the oil is sufficiently hot, add one bhatura.
  10. Press with the spoon to puff up.
  11. Also once they begin to puff, splash oil over the bhatura till they puff completely.
  12. Flip over and fry the bhatura till golden brown all over.
  13. Then drain the bhature into tissue paper to remove excess oil. Fry all bhature the same way.

    14. For Pressure Cooking Chole

  14. In a pressure cooker, add soaked chickpeas, dry amla, dry red chilli, black cardamom, salt to taste, baking soda, bay leaf, tea powder and water as required.
  15. Cover and pressure cook for 5 whistles. If you do not have tea bags, then you can prepare tea decoction and add it to the cooker.
  16. Once the pressure releases, open the cooker and discard the tea bags. keep aside.
  17. Strain the chloe and keep the remaining water for later use.

    18. For Chole Masala

  18. In a bowl, add Black cardamom, Black peppercorns, Green cardamom, Mace, Dry fenugreek leaves.
  19. Add Cinnamon stick, Nutmeg, Star anise, Cloves, Fenugreek seeds.
  20. Transfer this into the pan and dry roast until the spices turn aromatic.
    21. Add coriander powder, A pinch of asafoetida, Degi red chilli powder, Cumin powder and dry roast well.
  21. Transfer it into a grinder jar and grind it finely. Keep it aside for further use.

    22. For Tempering Chole

  22. In a kadai, heat ghee, add pressure cooked chole, prepared chole masala, black tamarind water.
  23. Mix everything well to make sure the chole doesn’t break.
  24. Adjust consistency by adding remaining chole water.
  25. Simmer for 15-20 minutes on low flame, or until chole absorbs all the flavour.
  26. At the end, add ginger and ghee.
  27. Garnished with ginger, ghee, green chillies, fried aloo, sliced onion and coriander sprig.
  28. Serve hot with fried bhature and green chutney.

    29. For Fried Aloo

  29. Heat oil in a kadai, add boiled & cubed potatoes and fry until crisp and golden brown from all the sides.
  30. Onces it’s crisp, transfer it into a bowl.
  31. Add salt to taste, degi red chilli powder, dry mango powder and toss it well.
  32. Keep aside for further use.
